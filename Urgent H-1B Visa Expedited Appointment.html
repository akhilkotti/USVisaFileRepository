URGENT H-1B VISA EXPEDITED APPOINTMENT GUIDE

This document outlines the required actions for securing an Expedited Appointment (EA) for H-1B visa stamping in India due to a critical medical emergency involving an immediate family member. Your immediate action is required due to the current inability to book a regular appointment slot.

CURRENT SITUATION: Since regular appointments are unavailable, you must use the Email Workaround to submit your Expedited Appointment request. Follow the steps precisely.

1.IMMEDIATE ACTION PLAN

  1. Complete Prerequisites (DS-160 & Fee): You must first complete and submit the online DS-160 Nonimmigrant Visa Application form and pay the required MRV visa fee (currently non-refundable). Print both confirmation receipts.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Gather and scan all documents listed in the checklist below, especially the official hospital letter and birth certificate.
  3. Execute Email Workaround: Draft and send a detailed email (using the template in Section 4) to the official support channel requesting the Emergency Appointment.
    • Official Support Email: Check the U.S. Visa Scheduling website's "Contact Us" page for the most current address (often `support-india@usvisascheduling.com`).
    • ATTACH ALL DOCUMENTS to this single email.
  4. Monitor Daily: After sending the email, log into the official U.S. Visa Scheduling website multiple times per day (especially 6 AM - 8 AM IST). Continue to search for available regular slots, as cancellations can occur and may open a path to booking before the email review is complete.
  5. Await Decision: Monitor your email (including spam/junk) for a decision from the Consulate (typically 1-5 business days). If approved, you will be instructed to log in and select an expedited interview date.

2.DOCUMENT CHECKLIST FOR EXPEDITED REQUEST

You must have digital copies of these documents ready to attach to your email request or upload to the online system.

Part A: Proof of Medical Emergency (Critical Evidence)

1. Official Doctor's/Hospital Letter
  • Must be on Hospital Letterhead, signed by the treating physician.
  • Must state the Father's Full Name and the critical/life-threatening nature of the illness.
  • Must explicitly state that the immediate presence of the son/daughter (applicant) is required.
2. Supporting Hospital Records
  • Copy of recent Hospital Admission/Discharge papers.
  • Recent Medical Reports/Test Results substantiating the critical condition.
3. Proof of Relationship
  • Color scan of the Applicant's Birth Certificate (listing the father's name).

Part B: Mandatory Visa Application & Employment Documents

4. Passport & Visa Documents
  • Color scan of the Passport Biographical Page.
  • Copy of the DS-160 Confirmation Page (with barcode).
  • Copy of the MRV Fee Payment Receipt.
5. H-1B Approval Notice (I-797)
  • Color scan of the Original Form I-797 (Notice of Action) for the approved H-1B petition.
6. Employment Documentation
  • Employment Verification Letter from your U.S. Employer/HR (confirming job, salary, and expectation to return).
  • Copies of the three most recent pay stubs.
