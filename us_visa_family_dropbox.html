US Visa – India

US Visa Dropbox – Family / Group Submission(Interview Waiver)

Guide for one appointment letter with multiple family members (for example: father, mother and child) and separate document sets for each person.

1. One Appointment Letter, Many Applicants

When you book a family / group Dropbox appointment from a single profile on the USTravelDocs website, the system generates one combined appointment confirmation letter.

✔ This single appointment letter lists the primary applicant and all family / group members. ✔ You can carry one printout (take 3 copies for safety). ✔ All applicants will submit documents in the same time slot and VAC.

2. Separate Document Sets for Each Family Member

Even though the appointment letter is shared, every applicant must have their own documents. Prepare one stack per person:

Applicant A (Primary)

  • Current passport
  • All old passports with US visas (if any)
  • DS-160 confirmation page
  • 1 US visa photo (50mm × 50mm, last 6 months)
  • Optional: MRV fee receipt

Applicant B (Family Member)

  • Current passport
  • All old passports with US visas (if any)
  • DS-160 confirmation page
  • 1 US visa photo (50mm × 50mm, last 6 months)
  • Optional: MRV fee receipt

Applicant C (Family Member)

  • Current passport
  • All old passports with US visas (if any)
  • DS-160 confirmation page
  • 1 US visa photo (50mm × 50mm, last 6 months)
  • Optional: MRV fee receipt
Keep each person’s documents in a separate small folder (for example: “Father”, “Mother”, “Son”). Do not mix passports or DS-160 pages.

3. MRV Fee Receipts for Family / Group Payments

For family appointments, the MRV fee is usually paid once for all applicants together. The system automatically links that single payment to all applicants in the group.

✔ You can carry one printout of MRV fee receipt. (take 3 copies for safety). ✔ One combined payment covers all family members. ✔ The appointment confirmation already contains the receipt numbers for each applicant.
✖ VAC staff do not ask for MRV receipts in most cases, because payment status is already linked in their system.

3. Do We Need to Bring Envelopes?

No. For Dropbox (Interview Waiver) in India, the Visa Application Center (VAC) provides and manages the envelopes.

✔ You only bring your documents. ✔ The VAC staff arrange the documents for each applicant and place them into the official envelopes with barcodes / tracking labels. ✔ Envelopes are then sealed and kept by the VAC for dispatch to the Embassy / Consulate.
✖ Do not seal your own envelopes at home and expect to submit them directly to the Consulate. Always follow VAC staff instructions.

4. Family Dropbox Visit – Step by Step

  1. Arrive at the VAC together for the slot shown on the family appointment letter. Carry passports, document folders for each person and at least one copy of the appointment confirmation.
  2. Security check & entry. Show the appointment letter and a passport (usually the primary applicant’s) at the entrance and complete security screening.
  3. Verification counter. Staff verify:
    • Names of all applicants on the appointment letter, and
    • DS-160 confirmation pages and passports for each family member.
  4. Envelope & barcodes. VAC staff provide envelopes or manage them at their counter. They attach barcodes / tracking labels for each applicant and arrange the documents in the correct order.
  5. Sealing & submission. The envelopes are sealed and retained by the VAC to be sent to the US Embassy / Consulate.
  6. Acknowledgment & tracking. You may receive an acknowledgment or be instructed to track your case using your passport number / DS-160 on the CEAC or USTravelDocs website.

5. Quick Family Checklist Before Leaving Home

  • Family / group appointment confirmation letter (1–3 copies)
  • Separate document folder for each family member
  • DS-160 confirmation pages for all applicants
  • Correct VAC location, date and time confirmed
  • US visa photos (50mm × 50mm) for each person
  • Old passports with US visas (if applicable)
  • Optional but safe: MRV fee receipt printouts / screenshots
This is a general helper guide for families using the US Visa Dropbox (Interview Waiver) option in India. Always follow the latest official instructions on the Embassy / Consulate and USTravelDocs websites.
