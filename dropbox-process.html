US Visa – India

US Visa Dropbox (Interview Waiver)

Quick guide to documents and the exact steps at the Visa Application Center (VAC). Use this as a printable checklist for each applicant.

1. What does “place in a single envelope for each applicant” mean?

It means that each applicant (father, mother, child, etc.) will have their own set of documents kept together and submitted in a separate official envelope at the VAC.

You do not need to buy or bring a special envelope from home. The VAC will provide the envelope or handle it at the counter.

2. Documents to prepare in advance Per Applicant

Mandatory Documents

  • Current passport (valid and undamaged)
  • Old passports that contain US visas (if any)
  • DS-160 confirmation page (with barcode)
  • Dropbox appointment letter (from ustraveldocs account)
  • 1 photo – US visa size (50mm x 50mm), taken within last 6 months

Optional but Recommended

  • MRV fee receipt (email printout or screenshot)
  • Copy of previous US visas (if passport is very old)
  • Simple folder to carry documents neatly
Arrange each person’s documents in a separate stack (for example: “Father stack”, “Mother stack”, “Child stack”). The VAC staff will guide you on placing them into the official envelope.

3. Before your Dropbox appointment day

  1. Complete the DS-160 form online and save the confirmation page.
  2. Create / log in to your ustraveldocs profile and add the applicant.
  3. Pay the MRV fee using the available payment method.
  4. Once the payment is activated, schedule a Dropbox appointment at your selected VAC location and print the appointment letter.
  5. Keep all documents in a simple folder, ready for each applicant.

4. What happens at the VAC on Dropbox day?

  1. Entrance & security: Show your passport and Dropbox appointment letter, complete security checks, and enter the VAC.
  2. Initial verification: Staff check your DS-160, passport, and appointment letter to confirm Interview Waiver (Dropbox) eligibility.
  3. Envelope handling: The VAC provides an official envelope or handles it at the counter. They may:
    • Ask you to place each applicant’s documents inside, or
    • Take the documents and arrange them themselves.
  4. Labelling & sealing: A label/tracking sticker linked to your case is attached. The envelope is then sealed by the staff.
  5. Acknowledgment: The VAC keeps the envelope and may give you an acknowledgment or instruct you to track status using your DS-160 / passport number.
You usually cannot hand over your own sealed envelope directly to the Consulate. Always follow the VAC staff instructions.

5. After submission – what next?

  • The VAC forwards your passport and documents to the US Embassy/Consulate for review.
  • You can track the status online via the CEAC website or ustraveldocs portal.
  • If the visa is approved, your passport is returned with the visa stamp.
  • In some cases, the Consulate may ask you to attend an in-person interview. If so, you will receive instructions to schedule it.

6. Quick checklist to review before you leave home

  • Correct VAC location and appointment date/time
  • Separate document stack for each applicant
  • DS-160 confirmation page for each person
  • Dropbox appointment letter printout
  • Photo: 50mm x 50mm, white background, taken in last 6 months
  • Old passports with US visas (if any)
  • Optional: MRV fee receipt printout or screenshot

This guide is for general understanding only. Always follow the latest official instructions from the US Embassy/Consulate and the ustraveldocs website.
